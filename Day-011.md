## ✅ Problems Solved

### 1. Valid Sudoku

**Platform:** LeetCode (LC 36)

**Approach:**

* Used three Hash Sets to track numbers in each row, column, and 3×3 sub-box.
* If a duplicate was found in any set, the Sudoku board was invalid.

**Time Complexity:** O(1) *(Board size is fixed: 9×9)*

**Space Complexity:** O(1)

---

### 2. Isomorphic Strings

**Platform:** LeetCode (LC 205)

**Approach:**

* Maintained two Hash Maps to ensure a one-to-one mapping between characters of both strings.

**Time Complexity:** O(n)

**Space Complexity:** O(n)

---

### 3. Word Pattern

**Platform:** LeetCode (LC 290)

**Approach:**

* Used two Hash Maps to maintain a unique mapping between pattern characters and words.

**Time Complexity:** O(n)

**Space Complexity:** O(n)

---

## 🧠 Key Learnings

* Hash Sets are excellent for detecting duplicates efficiently.
* Two-way Hash Maps help maintain one-to-one relationships.
* Matrix validation problems often rely on multiple Hash Sets.
* Hashing is one of the most frequently tested interview topics.
