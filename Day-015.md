---

✅ Problems Solved

1. Sort Colors

Platform: LeetCode (LC 75)

Approach:

- Used the Dutch National Flag Algorithm with three pointers.
- Partitioned the array into 0s, 1s, and 2s in a single pass.

Time Complexity: O(n)

Space Complexity: O(1)

---

2. Merge Sorted Array

Platform: LeetCode (LC 88)

Approach:

- Started from the end of both arrays.
- Compared elements and placed the larger one at the end.

Time Complexity: O(m + n)

Space Complexity: O(1)

---

3. Sort Array By Parity

Platform: LeetCode (LC 905)

Approach:

- Used Two Pointers to place even numbers before odd numbers.

Time Complexity: O(n)

Space Complexity: O(1)

---

🧠 Key Learnings

- Different sorting algorithms have different trade-offs in terms of time and space complexity.
- Python's built-in "sort()" uses Timsort, which has a time complexity of O(n log n) in the average and worst cases.
- Two Pointer techniques are commonly used in sorting-related interview problems.
- Sorting is often the first step in solving many array and greedy problems.
